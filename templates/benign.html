Breast Cancer: Benign

Not all abnormalities point towards Breast Cancer. Cruelly enough, the symptoms of benign cancer are very similar to that of malignant. These include but are not limited to pain or swelling in the breast region, abnormal lumps or change in colour.

Itâ€™s not all bad though. Statistics suggest that benign cancer cases occur more frequently than actual cancer. But it should be noted that benign cancer situations may pave the way to the eventual arrival of malignant cancer. Benign cancer is categorised into (the meaning of each phrase is self-explanatory):

  • No increase in risk
  • Slight increase in risk
  • Moderate increase in risk

Possible explanations in each category:

1. No increase in risk:
  • Breast Cysts
  • Calcifications
  • Fibroadenoma
  • Central Intraductal Papilloma

2. Slight increase in risk:
  • Adenosis
  • Radial Scars
  • Fibromatosis
  • Flat Epithelial Atypia

3. Moderate increase in risk:
  • Atypical Ductal Hyperplasia
  • Lobular Neoplasia

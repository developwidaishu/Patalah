Breast Cancer: Malignant

Early detection plays a big role in curing cancer. The earlier a tumour is detected the better a prognosis will be. It is also imperative to note that a malignant cancer tumour is not a death warrant, chances of survival do exist.

The next step is to classify the stage of cancer. It is usually expressed in numbers 0 - IV. The stage of cancer is determined through a surgery where a certain amount of underarm lymph nodes are examined. Based on the following measurements the stage number is determined

  • Tumour size/whether or not it has grown into the nearby tissue (T)
  • Whether cancer is in the lymph nodes (N)
  • Whether cancer has spread to other parts of the body (M)
  • Tumour grade
  • Estrogen-progestron receptor status
  • HER-2 Status
  • Oncotype DX score

The stage may also be described as local, regional or distant depending on where the cancer has spread.

In stage-0 the cancer cells are confined to where they started i.e. they haven’t spread to neighbouring regions of the body (includes DCIS).

Treatment:

  • Total Masectomy
  • Lumpectomy (with/without radiation)
  • No chemotherapy required
  • No treatment to lymph nodes required
  • Hormonal therapy can be done

In Stage 1, invasive breast cancer cells have spread to other breast tissues. Divided into 1A and 1B.

In Stage 1A the cancer has not spread to remaining tissues and tumour size is <=2 cm.

In Stage 1B cancer cells (0.2 mm - 2mm) are found in lymph nodes. No cancer tumour exists or there is a tumour (<= 2cm).

Treatment:

  • Total Masectomy
  • Lumpectomy (with/without radiation)
  • No chemotherapy required
  • No treatment to lymph nodes required
  • Hormonal therapy can be done

Stage 2 is divided into two subcategories 2A and 2B.

2A is charecterised by either an absence of tumour however cancer cells are found in a few lymph nodes or the tumour measures <=2cm and has spread to lymph nodes or is>2 cm and <=5 cm and has not spread to lymph nodes.

In 2B the tumor is between 2 cm to 5 cm in size; small groups of breast cancer cells (0.2 mm to 2 mm are found in the lymph nodes). The cancer may have spread to 1 to 3 lymph nodes. It may also be possible that the tumor is larger than 5 cm but has not spread to the lymph nodes.

Treatment:

  • Total Masectomy
  • Lumpectomy with radiation
  • Chemotherapy
  • Sentinel Lymph node biopsy
  • Axillary Lymph node biopsy
  • Hormonal Therapy
  • Targeted therapy may be used

Stage 3 is divided into 3A, 3B and 3C. Either no tumor is found in the breast or the tumor may be of any size; cancer is found in 4 to 9 lymph nodes or in the lymph nodes.

The tumor is >5 cm; small groups of breast cancer cells (0.2 mm-2 mm) are found in the lymph nodes,

The tumor is >5 cm; cancer has spread to 1 to 3 lymph nodes.

Treatment:

  • Total Masectomy with radiation
  • Chemotherapy
  • Lymph node removal
  • Hormonal therapy
  • Targeted therapy

The last stage of Breast Cancer is characterised by the spread of cancer cells to other regions of the body. These include lungs, skin, bones, lymph nodes or brain.

Commonly used terms for this stage are “de novo”, “advanced” or “metastatic”.

Treatment:

  • Surgery
  • Radiation
  • Treatment of enlarged lymph nodes
  • Chemotherapy
  • Hormonal Therapy
  • Radiation for other parts of the body
  • Targeted Therapy
